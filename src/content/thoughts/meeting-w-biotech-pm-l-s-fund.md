Refresher on PIPEs, Convertible Notes, & Structured Private Credit

##### PIPES

PIPEs involve a public company selling equity or equity-linked paper to a negotiated group of institutions, off-market, usually at a discount to the prevailing price. Cash burn is biotech is quite high and companies like to raise selectively around catalysts – a fund can also build a position larger than it could accumulate in the open market without moving the tape.

Mechanics:

* Pricing is off a reference (last close, or a trailing VWAP), at a discount that compensates for illiquidity, block-size, and lock-up – call it 5-20% in most deals, tighter or event at-the-market in hotter ones. The discount size is the core negotiation.
* Registration rights: the shares are restricted, so the issuer commits to file a resale registration and get it effective within a set window, with liquidated damages if they're late.
* Wall-crossing.
* Structured Beyond Common Shares: convertible preferred, common + warrants, or a registered direct (already-registered shares, faster). Warrant coverage is the standard sweetner.
* PFWs – biotech-specific and worth knowing. Investor pays essentially the full price upfront, exercise price nominal, but it sits as a warrant so it doesn't count toward beneficial-ownership blockers.

**The PIPE discount and any structured conversion terms are priced off the fund's *private estimate of where the asset is going* – i.e., it's milestone view. A verified probability is an input to the discount the investor demands.**



* *"You price CVRs. A CVR is just a securitized milestone bet — and when it trades, its price is a market-implied probability of that milestone hitting."*

* *"But a CVR only exists because an M&A deal minted it. The probability discovery is a byproduct of the transaction."*

* *"Every other milestone in biotech has the identical structure — binary event, huge value riding on it — but no deal ever minted a vehicle, so no market, no price. The probability is just as real; it's invisible."*

* *"VSA mints that vehicle deliberately, for any milestone, without needing an acquisition. CVR-style price discovery for the assets that never get a CVR."*

  *"The hard part is that a CVR borrows its credibility from the deal behind it. Mine has no deal — so I have to manufacture the credibility the CVR gets for free. That's what the neutrality and verifiability work is for."*
